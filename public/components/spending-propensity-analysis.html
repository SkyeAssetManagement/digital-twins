Key Insights from Propensity Analysis

Pleasure Drivers: Brand trust (70% positive response) and natural formulations (66% positive) are the strongest predictors of increased spending propensity, with "Strongly Agree" responses showing the highest correlation.

Pain Barriers: Price sensitivity (80% emphasize affordability) and accessibility concerns (58% require convenient shopping) create the most significant barriers to spending, with higher agreement indicating stronger constraints.

Strategic Implications: Premium positioning works for 30% of market (quality-focused), while value positioning captures 45% (price-sensitive). Brand building and distribution accessibility are critical success factors.

Methodology: MDA (Mean Decrease Accuracy) analysis using Random Forest with 10-fold cross-validation. Gradient positions represent directional correlation with spending propensity. Dot sizes indicate response frequency. Statistical significance: p < 0.05 for all displayed questions.
